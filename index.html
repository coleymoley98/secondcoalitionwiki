With a smooth pull of a charging handle and an ever more unpredicatable climate war has returned to North America. The Potomac and Shenedoah rivers have not been tainted by blood since the early 19th century, but now Canadian forces build up a new defensive line in preperation for America's last dying stab at glory. Out west the Colorado and Rio Grande rivers have become vital vital to Califrornian agricultores thanks to techniques imported from Israel. This is in stark contrast to Texas' plans to introduce a hydroelectric system modelled on ColumbiaHydro, a project which will dam the Rio Grande at five different places. As droughts and dust storms transition from disasters to seasonal weather every drop of water is precious and the two allies will not hesitate to shed blood.
