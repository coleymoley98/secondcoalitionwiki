UNITED PROVINCES OF CANADA 

last edited
March 31, 2021 10:39 PM

The United Provinces of Canada (UPC), commonly known as the United Provinces (U.P. or UP), Canada, or the Provinces is a country primarily located in North America. It consists of 24 provinces, six major self-governing territories, and some minor possessions.[g] At 3.8 million square miles (9.8 million square kilometers), it is the world's third- or fourth-largest country by total area.[c] With a population of more than 256 million people, it is the third most populous country in the world. The national capital is Kingston, Ontario, and the most populous city is Toronto. Paleo-Indians migrated from Siberia to the North American mainland at least 12,000 years ago, and European colonization began in the 16th century. The United Provinces reemerged from British colonies established in Northeastern America. Disputes over taxation and political representation with Great Britain led to the American Revolutionary War (1775–1783), which established independence until the Anglo-American War (1811-13) which saw the United States cede all land north of the Potomac River
